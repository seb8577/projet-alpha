GOOD HOPE CAPE

The Cape of Good Hope1 is a rocky promontory on the Atlantic coast of South Africa, at the tip of the Cape Peninsula south of Cape Town, which closes False Bay to the west. This rocky promontory ends at Cape Point, 2 km from the Cape of Good Hope proper. It is a nature reserve covered by coastal paths.

GOOD HOPE CAPE

The Cape of Good Hope is located at the junction of two very different maritime currents, a cold current "Benguela" to the west and a hot current, the current of Aiguilles, to the east. It is not the southernmost point of Africa, the southernmost point of this continent being the Cape of Aiguilles further south-east.

KRISTERNBOSCHE GARDEN

The Kirstenbosch Botanical Garden is located in the most affluent part of the suburbs of Cape Town in South Africa. This botanical garden is one of the most renowned in the world thanks to its collections and its exceptional location. The garden, founded in 1913, currently occupies an area of 528 hectares.

STELLENBOSCHE

Located about fifty kilometers from the city of Cape Town, at the edge of the Eerste Rivier, the city of oaks (Eikestad in Afrikaans and Dutch) is famous for its university, its Dutch architecture, its wine industry and for the Springboks. It was also an important center of Afrikaner nationalism during the twentieth century.

GANSBAAI

Gansbaai is a fishing village located in the south of the Western Cape province in South Africa. It is a known tourist destination for the presence on its coasts of a dense population of White sharks and a favorable place for their observation.
