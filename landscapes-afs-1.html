BREED RIVER

Breede River Valley is a region of Western Cape Province, South Africa known for being the largest fruit and wine producing valley in the Western Cape, as well as South Africa's leading race-horse breeding area. It is part of the Boland bordering on becoming Little Karoo towards the east.

BREED RIVER

The Breede River Valley is relatively broad and flat for a Western Cape valley, averaging at a floor height of 80m-250m above sea-level. Western regions are mostly alluvial and flat, while eastern regions have more hills of the Bokkeveld Group with narrow alluvial deposits.

TABLE MOUNTAIN

Table Mountain is a clump of the Western Cape Province of South Africa, overlooking Cape Town. It is one of the seven new wonders of nature. Its name comes from its shape which, from a distance, seems as flat as the top of a table. When the clouds cling, it is said that the "tablecloth" is put.

CAPE TOWN

The city of Cape Town, founded in 1652, is considered the mother city of South Africa. The southernmost city on the African continent, it is established on the banks of Table Bay and is topped by Table Mountain with two peaks named Lion's Head and Devil's Peak. The city was named after the Cape of Good Hope.

LION'S HEAD

Lion's Head is a mountain in Cape Town, South Africa, between Table Mountain and Signal Hill. Lion's Head peaks at 669 metres (2,195 ft) above sea level. The peak forms part of a dramatic backdrop to the city of Cape Town and is part of the Table Mountain National Park.
