SERENGETI

Serengeti National Park is a large national park located in northern Tanzania covering 14,763 km2. It is the second animal park of Africa; about four million animals of which more than 400 species of birds are present. He is best known for his two annual migrations of millions of wildebeest, zebra and Thomson gazelles.

TARANGIRE

Baobabs, an unusual tree in northern Tanzania, abound in the northern part of the park. The landscape consists mainly of savannah, with many acacias, and becomes more marshy in the South. Note also the presence of many termite mounds, whether isolated or leaning against the trunks of trees.

NGORONGORO CRATER

The Ngorongoro crater is located in northern Tanzania, west of Arusha, in the Ngorongoro district of Arusha region. It is part of the Ngorongoro Massif, an area of plateaus and volcanic peaks that stretches from Lake Eyasi to Lake Natron and is framed by the Serengeti plain to the northwest by the volcanic mountains of Mount Meru, Kilimanjaro and Monduli.

NGORONGORO CRATER

The Ngorongoro crater is a vast circular caldera more than twenty kilometers in diameter located in the heart of the Ngorongoro Massif in northern Tanzania, in the eastern branch of the Great Rift Valley. Ngorongoro is the largest intact and undewatered caldera in the world with an area of 326 km2.
