LION

The male lion only occasionally hunts, he is responsible for fighting intrusions on the territory and threats against the troop. Significant populations of African lions are located in the national parks of Kenya, Tanzania and South Africa.

LIONESS

Lionesses and lions use different techniques depending on the terrain, their preferences and methods of defending prey. The lioness hunts in general at dawn or dusk, or during the night. As lionesses hunt in open spaces, the common hunt increases the chance of successfully hitting prey.

LIONESSES ON THE TREE

After a gestation of about four months, the lioness, hidden away from the group, gives birth to one to four lion cubs. During their first six weeks of life, they will only be breastfed by the mother in the cache by her four mammary glands. If the latter is far enough away from the group, the mother will go alone to the hunt.

VERVET MONKEY

It lives on the edge of wooded areas and in the African savannahs, Senegal, Somalia, Central African Republic, South Africa, for example. It is abundant in the park of Niokolo-Koba in Senegal. Its population is estimated at 30,000 individuals. Lives in groups of 20 to 50.

ZEBRA

They are mainly in Central and Southern Africa. These animals are characterized by bands of stripes, generally vertical, black and white. They are found in a variety of habitats, such as grasslands, savannahs, forests, thorny scrublands, mountains and coastal hills.

WASP'S NEST

This bird has bright green plumage and a reddish-red throat with yellow only on the chin. His median rectrices are very long. It nests alone or in loose colonies in the plains and sandy slopes and in the ditches. The nest is established at the bottom of a tunnel of one to three meters.
