ZEBRA

They are mainly in Central and Southern Africa. These animals are characterized by bands of stripes, generally vertical, black and white. They are found in a variety of habitats, such as grasslands, savannahs, forests, thorny scrublands, mountains and coastal hills.

WHITE RHINOCEROS

The southern white rhinoceros once lived in a belt from Angola and Namibia to Mozambique and KwaZulu-Natal through Zimbabwe and Botswana. Today, it is found in many reserves in South Africa. In 2005, there are 11,320 in the wild, and 740 in captivity (where it breeds).

SPRINGBOKS

The Springbok is a jumping antelope from southern Africa whose name Afrikaans means: 'spring'.At a symbol in South Africa, he is also the fetish animal of the national rugby team. The animal is also present in Namibia, Botswana and Angola.

LION

The male lion only occasionally hunts, he is responsible for fighting intrusions on the territory and threats against the troop. Significant populations of African lions are located in the national parks of Kenya, Tanzania and South Africa.

GREAT WHITE SHARK

With a maximum size greater than 6 m in length, it is one of the largest predatory fish currently living in the oceans. Great white shark habitat is mainly coastal in temperate waters, but has also been observed in epipelagic zones in the ocean. He is a lover of shallow waters.
