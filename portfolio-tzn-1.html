BLACK RHINOCEROS

The Ngorongoro crater is currently home to 30 black rhinoceros. The black rhino prefers the thorny shrub savannah or the forest edges. The presence of water points available nearby is necessary for both of them.

BUFFALO

Nearly one million buffaloes live on the African continent. Slower than other herbivores, they are very powerful and defend themselves by charging with their horns. African buffaloes are gregarious, irascible and very dangerous, able to kill lions or hunters.

GIRAFFE

The giraffe lives in Africa, in the savannah. It is found from Chad to South Africa. But it is mainly in Kenya, Tanzania, Botswana and Niger that one can meet herds of giraffes. This is the largest current animal in height, which can reach up to 5.50 m or even 5.80 m thanks to the length of its neck.

ELEPHANT

The African savanna elephant; it measures about 4 meters at the withers, has two prehensile "fingers" at the end of the horn and large ears to regulate its internal temperature. The forest elephant also lives in Africa, with ears generally smaller and more circular than the savannah elephant, and thinner and upright tusks.

CROCODILE OF THE NIL

The average length of the adult is 4 meters but we have observed some individuals that exceeded 7 meters. The bite force of the Nile crocodile is in the order of 2.2 tons-force. This species is found in most parts of Africa (with the exception of North Africa, Seychelles and Comoros).

JACKAL

Jackal is the vernacular name for several small and medium-sized species of the Canidae family. Like the wolf and the dog, they belong to the genus Canis. They are found in Africa, Asia but also in Mediterranean Europe and more particularly in the Balkans.
