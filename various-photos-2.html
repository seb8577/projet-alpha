CANADIAN GREY WOLF

Under its wild forms, the gray wolf was gradually exterminated by humans in several areas of its original range, especially in the nineteenth century. In the twenty-first century it remains present mainly in wilderness areas such as the Siberian and Canadian Taiga or the steppes and mountain ranges of Central Eurasia.

CANADIAN GREY WOLF

The male usually has larger dimensions than the female. The total length of the muzzle to the end of the tail is on average 1.65 m for the male and 1.59 m for the female. The height at the withers ranges from 66 to 81 cm. Adult gray wolves weigh an average of 16 to 50 kg for females and 20 to 70 kg.

EUROPEAN OTTER

She lives in the mountains, up to an altitude of 1,300 m. It is usually solitary, occupying an area of 5 to 15 km of shoreline along a river (sometimes more) or 20 to 30 km2 in marsh area. She regularly borrows the same passages on the bank to get into the water: the "flows".

SCROLL MOON

The length of the half major axis between the Moon and the Earth is 384 399 km. The period of rotation of the Moon is the same as its orbital period and so it always has the same hemisphere (named "visible face of the moon") to a terrestrial observer (the other hemisphere is called the "face of the moon").
