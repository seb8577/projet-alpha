BLACK PENGUIN

These birds live in colonies on 24 islands between Namibia and Algoa Bay, near Port Elizabeth, South Africa. The largest colony is on Dyer Island near Kleinbaai. There are also two colonies on the mainland, one at Boulders Beach, near Cape Town, the other at Stony Point near Betty's Bay, between Cape Town and Hermanus.

CHEETAH

The cheetah's cry can sometimes be likened to a bird's scream, but also to the mewing of a cat. When the cheetah shows his anger, he fires. The cheetah can not roar, because it has complete ossification of the hyoid bone like animals of the genus Felis.

CHEETAH

Cheetah is a large carnivorous mammal of the family Felidae living in Africa and West Asia. The cheetah has a slender and fine look, its coat is entirely spotted with black on a tawny to very light beige background. He is considered the fastest land animal in the world, with a speed of up to 112 kms/h.

ELEPHANT

The African savanna elephant; it measures about 4 meters at the withers, has two prehensile "fingers" at the end of the horn and large ears to regulate its internal temperature. The forest elephant also lives in Africa, with ears generally smaller and more circular than the savannah elephant, and thinner and upright tusks.

HIPPOPOTAMUS

They are massive aquatic animals with a barrel-shaped body. Many species have disappeared but there are still two alive: the best known species, the hippopotamus said amphibious, and the hippopotamus dwarf. Their life expectancy is around forty years.
